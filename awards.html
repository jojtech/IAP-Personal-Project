Wangari Maathai Forest Champion Award

The Wangarĩ Maathai Forest Champion Award, launched in 2012 by the Collaborative Partnership on Forests (CPF), honors individuals making significant contributions to international forest issues. The award recognizes champions who have demonstrated exemplary efforts in forest conservation and sustainability. Past winners include Narayan Kaji Shrestha (2012), Martha Isabel "Pati" Ruiz Corzo (2014), Gertrude Kabusimbi Kenyangi (2015), Maria Margarida Ribeiro da Silva (2017), Léonidas Nzigiyimpa (2019), and Cécile Ndjebet (2022). Each recipient, representing diverse regions, has played a crucial role in forestry activism, showcasing the global impact of their work.

Posthumous recognition

Posthumous recognition for Wangarĩ Maathai includes the establishment of Wangarĩ Gardens in Washington, DC, a 2.7-acre community garden project honoring her commitment to environmental protection and community engagement. The University of Pittsburgh dedicated the Wangarĩ Maathai Trees and Garden, featuring red maples symbolizing her environmental dedication and a circular flower garden representing her global vision. A statue was unveiled at her alma mater, Benedictine College, in 2014, and a mural was added during the 2019 renovation of Westerman Hall. Nairobi renamed Forest Road to Wangarĩ Maathai Road in 2016, recognizing her efforts against deforestation. UNESCO published a graphic novel in 2015, depicting her story as part of their series on Women in African History. These diverse tributes underscore Maathai's enduring influence on environmental activism, education, and community empowerment.
